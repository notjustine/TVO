Finding Cyberbullying Help

Remember, you are not alone. If you are being cyberbullied and you need help, reach out to a trusted adult such as a parent, teacher, or school counselor. On most social media sites and apps, you can also report and block specific people that may be harassing you.

If you or somoene you know is being cyberbullied, below are important resources for help:
• Kids Help Phone
• Cybertip.ca
• Canadian Centre for Child Protection

What if someone I know is being cyberbullied?

If you think or know someone is being bullied, try and stand up for them! Don't be a bystander.

Similar to if you were being cyberbullied, help them find a trusted adult to talk to and report the bullying. It may also help them to save any messages received as evidence.

Most importantly, it goes a long way to remind them that they are not alone and that they can trust you to be by their side.

Tips for Online Safety and Privacy

The best way to lessen your chances of experiencing cyberbullying is to practice online safety and privacy.

1. Protect your privacy with passwords.
Never give out your passwords, even to your closest friends.

2. Remember that things you post may not be private.
Once it is posted on the Internet, anyone can see it and save it, even if you delete it after.

3. Respect your friends' privacy.
Once you post it, it's out there for all to see—you can apologize, but you can't take it back.

4. Protect your privacy by using a made-up name.
Not all places onlike are right to use your real name. For example, a school library is different from a streaming site.

5. Ask a parent or guardian for help or permission.

Be kind online, and remind yourself that you do not deserve to be bullied or harassed.
