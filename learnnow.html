What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying involves the use of communication technologies or electronic media to bully others by embarassment, exclusion, intimidation, threats or harassment. It can happen on Websites and social networking sites, or using email, text messaging or instant messaging.

Some common examples of cyberbullying are:
• Sending mean or threatening messages to others
• Sharing personal/embarassing pictures of others without permission
• Impersonation and creating fake accounts.

How is it different from traditional bullying?
Unlike traditional bullying such as physical or verbal, cyberbullying affects victims in different ways since it can follow a victim anywhere. It reaches victims in places that are usually safe from traditional forms of bullying. The cyberbully can be anonymous, and therefore may tend to do so and say more hurtful things thanks to being able to "hide behind a screen." Additionally, this anonymity means that the cyberbully does not see the victim's reaction and pain, making it difficult for cyberbullies to recognize that they are hurting someone.

With kids being exposed to technology and various social media at earlier and earlier ages, cyberullying awareness is critical. Rather than confiscating their devices or limiting their overall exposure to the Internet, education in online safety and privacy is vital so that kids can practice and develop good Internet safety skills from a young age.
